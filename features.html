Introduction

Africa is a continent rich in culture, values, and traditions. Among its diverse peoples, the Yoruba culture of West Africa stands out for its deep sense of morality, respect, and etiquette. From ancient times, Yoruba people have placed great emphasis on moral behavior, respect for elders, honesty, and communal living. These values are taught from childhood through proverbs, stories, and everyday interactions that shape character and promote harmony in society.

Yoruba morals and etiquette teach respect, discipline, and integrity. Elders are highly respected, and greetings are considered signs of good upbringing. Children are taught to show humility and politeness in speech and behavior, as these values strengthen family and community ties.
